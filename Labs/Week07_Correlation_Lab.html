Experiment Overview


In this experiment, you recruited a sample of adolescents who participated in team sports. Participants have an average score for each of 4 measures associated with desire for power and achievement. These scores, when totaled together, will provide a measure that reflects a participant’s self-enhancement values. Higher scores on this composite metric reflect stronger self-enhancement values. Participants also have an Antisocial Behaviour score, which reflects their average score on the antisocial items from the Prosocial and Antisocial Behavior in Sport Scale. Higher scores on this scale reflect a larger number of endorsed antisocial behaviours.

Run Correlations

As you are testing for an association between two continuous variables, you’ll use a correlation to provide statistical support for your hypotheses.

Recall that \(r\) has the following interpretation standards:

Strength Magnitude of \(r\)
Negligible \(<\) .1
Weak .1 - .3
Moderate .3 - .6
Strong \(>\) .6

To correlate SelfEnhancement with AntisocialBehaviour, navigate to Analyze > Correlate > Bivariate and add both of these to the ‘Variables’ box. You can specify that you’d like to also compute the relevant confidence intervals by selecting ‘Confidence interval’ box and ticking the ‘Estimate confidence intervals of the bivariate correlation parameter’ box. Click ‘Continue’ and then ‘OK’. You’ll get the following output:

The top box provides a correlation matrix with the value of \(r\) and the p-value, while the bottom box gives the confidence interval associated with the correlation. Here, you can see that \(r\) = .3, which equates to a weak to moderate association between SelfEnhancement and AntisocialBehaviour. The \(p\)-value (< .001) is less than the \(\alpha\) threshold of .05, so this association is significant. The 95% confidence interval indicates that we can be 95% confident that the true association (\(r\)) between these two variables falls between .17 and .44.

You can correlate the SE variables in the same way as above. This time, click ‘Show only the lower diagonal’. This removes redundant information from the correlation matrix, which is how correlation matrices are typically presented:

Here, you can see that all variables are significantly associated with each other, which you would expect given that they are supposed to measure the same construct.
